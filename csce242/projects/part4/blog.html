Staying Hydrated, Staying Healthy

When the temperatures rise, getting enough water is important whether you;re playing sports, traveling, or just sitting in the sun. It's critical for your heart health Drinking enough water keeps the body hydrated, which helps the heart more easily pump blood through the body. It also helps muscles work efficiently. If you're well hydrated, your heart doesn't have to work as hard. Dehydration means your body doesn't have enough water, which can make you feel really thirsty and cause other problems such as dizziness, feeling tired, swollen feet, a headache or even life-threatening illnesses, such as heat stroke.
